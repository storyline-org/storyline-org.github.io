Audiobook management, reimagined.

Managing a digital audiobook library shouldn't be this hard. Files scattered across folders, inconsistent metadata, missing cover art, no way to track your progress—it's chaos. Calibre solved this for ebooks years ago, giving readers a powerful tool to organize, edit, and enjoy their collections. Audiobook listeners deserve the same.

Introducing Storyline

Storyline is that solution. Think Calibre, but purpose-built for audio. Beautiful cover art, smart organization by series and author, playback progress tracking—everything you need in one place.

Your audiobooks deserve better than a folder full of M4B files. Storyline gives them the home they've been waiting for.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is Storyline for?

Storyline is for anyone who collects and listens to audiobooks. Whether you have a small collection or thousands of titles, if you've ever felt frustrated by the lack of good audiobook management tools, Storyline is for you.

Is Storyline an Electron app?

No. Storyline is built using GPUI, a GPU-accelerated Rust framework. This means truly native performance, minimal memory usage, and a responsive interface that feels fast and fluid.

What platforms does Storyline support?

Currently, Storyline is available for macOS only during the beta period. We're planning to expand to Windows and Linux after the initial release.

What audio formats are supported?

Storyline supports all common audiobook formats including M4B, M4A, MP3, FLAC, OGG, and more. We handle both single-file audiobooks and multi-file collections.

Where is my library data stored?

Your library metadata is stored locally on your machine. Your actual audiobook files stay exactly where you put them—Storyline never moves or modifies your original files without your permission.

Is Storyline open source?

Not yet. During the beta period, Storyline is closed source. We're planning to open source the project at release. We believe in transparency and community-driven development.
